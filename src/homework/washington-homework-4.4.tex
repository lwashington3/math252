\pdfbookmark[2]{Supplemental Problem}{prb:supplemental}
Suppose that you are given a $6^{\mbox{th}}$ order linear differential equation with constant coefficients \[ a_{6}y^{(6)} + a_{5}y^{(5)} + \dots + a_{1}y' + a_{0}y = f(x) \] and that the auxiliary equation of the complementary differential equation can be written in factored form as \[ m^{3}(m+3)(m-(2+5i))(m-(2-5i))=0 \]
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\textbf{\roman*})]
    \supproblem{prb:supplemental-1} Write the general solution $y_{c}$ of the complementary differential equation.
\end{enumerate}

In each part below, a different function $f(x)$ is given for the above differential equation. Give the form of a particular solution $y_{p}$ of the DE that one should look for according to the method of Undetermined Coefficients. (Do not attempt to solve for the coefficients)
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\textbf{\roman*}),start=2]
    \supproblem{prb:supplemental-2} \[ f(x) = e^{-3x} \]
	\supproblem{prb:supplemental-3} \[ f(x) = 4x + 7 \]
	\supproblem{prb:supplemental-4} \[ f(x) = \cos(6x) \]
	\supproblem{prb:supplemental-5} \[ f(x) = e^{2x}\sin(5x) \]
	\supproblem{prb:supplemental-6} \[ f(x) = xe^{-3x} \]
\end{enumerate}
